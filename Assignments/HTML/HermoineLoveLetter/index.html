Loveletter to my Ronald

Dear Ronald,

I know we have been friends for a long time, and I'd hate to see our friendship dissolve after this.
But this has been on my mind a lot lately, and I'm afraid to say, that I must tell you how I really feel.


I love you, to say the least.
I like how you are so cute and clumsy on your broomstick, and I like how your many freckles
sometimes resemble chicken pox so much, that I actually wonder if you are contagious.
Not that you are, of course.
After all, they're just freckles.


Anyways, I haven't time to spare to tell you this in person.
I've just been so caught up with schooling and McGonagall is making me take extra classes.
I know you don't exactly care for school, Ronald, but you could at least take the time to show up for classes.
I'm telling you this because the last time you chose not to show up, Flitwick threatened to fail you.


Anyways, I'm getting terribly off of the topic.


If you wish to continue being friends, I am absolutely fine with that.
After all, I don't want to make things awkward, especially since Harry didn't do so well with Cho.
Speaking of Harry, have you noticed how he's been depressed lately?
I think it's because of Sirius.
After all, it is the anniversary of his death tomorrow.
I propose we take him out for a butterbeer sometime soon to cheer him up.
What do you think about that?


Like I was saying before, no hard feelings about this okay?
But if you do happen to feel the same way about me, would you please let me know?
I realize you broke up with Lavender lately, so I know you don't have a girlfriend.
That is probably the only reason I've worked up the courage to actually write this.


So, Ron, please reply. Whether it is a yes or no, I'm fine with either.
But please, don't tell Harry about this latest affair.
I still want things to be normal between us.


My love and condolences,
Hermione
