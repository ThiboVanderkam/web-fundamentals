3 potion drinks

We’re making 3 of the drinks featured in Harry Potter! Pumpkin Fizz, Butterbeer and Polyjuice Potion!
Never been to the Wizarding World of Harry Potter? No problem - now you can have it in your kitchen!
ALL RECIPES & PRODUCTS USED ARE LISTED BELOW!

Recipe 1 Harry Potter Pumpkin Fizz Recipe

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 ½ cups pure pumpkin puree (NOT pumpkin pie filling)
  • 1 cup apricot juice
  • 1 ½ cups vanilla cream soda
  • 2 cups apple cider (non-alcoholic)
  • 1 ½ cup ginger ale
  • ice (optional – I didn’t use it)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Combine the pumpkin puree and apricot juice in a bowl or large measuring cup. Pour through a cheesecloth into a pumpkin cauldron.
  2. Add the cream soda, apple cider, ginger ale and ice and mix. Enjoy!

Recipe 2 Harry Potter Frozen Butterbeer Recip

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup vanilla ice cream
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup cream soda
  • 2 pumps caramel syrup
  • 1 pumps toffee nut syrup
  • whipped cream
  • caramel drizzle
  • edible gold stars

DIRECTIONS

  1. Blend ice cream, milk, cream soda, caramel syrup and toffee nut syrup in a blender.
  2. Squeeze some caramel drizzle into a tall glass. Pour the butterbeer into the glass. Top with whipped cream, caramel drizzle and edible gold stars.

Recipe 3 Harry Potter Polyjuice Potion Recipe

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cans frozen limeade
  • water
  • 1 litre (4 cups) sprite
  • green food coloring (optional)
  • handful of lacewing flies (raisins tossed with edible glitter)
  • 3 leeches (black licorice)
  • 1 horn of a Bicorn (drinking horn filled with sprite)
  • 2 reeds of knotgrass (candy seaweed tied into knot)
  • handful of fluxweed (cherry gummy stems and gummy frogs)
  • 2 pieces of Boomslang skin (flattened yellow and orange starburst)
  • gummy eyeball
  • Silver sugar stars

DIRECTIONS

  1. Pour the limeade into a bowl. Add 4 cans worth of water into the bowl. Add the sprite.
  2. Add the lacewing flies, leeches, horn of a Bicorn, knotgrass, fluxweed and Boomslang skin. Add a procured part of the individual who you wish to turn into.
    We’re using an eyeball, which is arguably a trickier body part to procure. You could also try toenail clippings, dandruff – anything you can think of!
  3. Add some green food coloring, if desired.
  4. Stir, then pour into vials. Top with silver stars and enjoy!
